Web Mockup

Site design created as part of a technical interview for a front-end designer position.

Brief: Imagine you're working with a client who is the largest eye care company in the greater Chicago area with 40+ locations.

  • They focus on eye exams, glasses, and contact lenses.
  • Soon, they will be opening up a brand new location in a suburb just outside of Chicago and need our help generating awareness and getting new patients to schedule appointments.
  • There will be a Facebook ad campaign with the following promotion: First 100 people to schedule an appointment get a free eye exam and 30% off their glasses frames.
  • The Facebook ad is going to lead to a landing page where the call to action is to get people to sign up for an appointment.
  • Design and write the copy for this landing page that will convince page visitors to sign up for this limited time offer.
  • Make up a name for the company.

We Can Code IT: Bootcamp Capstone Project

Collaborated remotely with three of my peers to build web application that tracks and plans meals using full tech stack.

  • Researched meal planning trends and leading reasons why they fail
  • Gathered data on cooking at home trend (increases related to COVID-19)
  • Found data related to food waste and rise of home-delivered meal kits
  • Created the logo, color scheme, and general site layout from scratch -- without using any frameworks
  • Used green and orange color scheme because they are positively related to food and health
  • Included features, like calendar reminders and grocery list generator, to help users commit to a meal plan and reduce food waste
  • Utilized remote collaboration tools like Google Meet, GitHub, and Slack to stay in communication and on task
Tech:

Java, Spring, Hibernate, JPA, JavaScript, MVC, HTML, CSS, Flexbox, Grid, TDD, Agile (Scrum), OOP, AJAX, JSON, RESTful API, Responsive Design / Mobile, Relational Databases, Source Control / Git, Kanban
